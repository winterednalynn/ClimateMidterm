Climate Crisis in the Bahamas

Bahama's Climate

            The location of the Bahama is placed in the boundry of the tropical and 
            subtropical zones and posesses a semi-tropical or subtropical marine climate. 
            Bahama experiences warm and humid conditions all year-round.
            The most warmest humidity presents in June - November. 
            There were dry periods in the years of 2004 - 2006.

Climate Crisis

            In 1990-1999 there were 43 hurricanes that impacted the Bahamas. It is concluded that the island 
            is predicted to be hit with hurricanes 4.3 times per decade. The Bahama Island is prone to be 
            jolted by storm surges which can outturn in flooding. When coastal flooding occurs there are consequences of 
            sea level rising. Additionally, the island's soil is predominately dry which calculates vulnerability to 
            drought when temperture increases and decreased of rainfall.

Severe flooding happens in the Bahamas. Hurricane Dorian caused an awful flooding for the country which killed 20 people and 70,000 lost homes.

Low preception in the Bahamas creates the country is at risk for a drought episode. This effects the country's wildlife and water quality, .

Sea life and Agriculture are highly dependent on climate. Bahamians experienced lack of constant access to adequate food due to weak agriculture.


Technology to the rescue

To assist Bahama is developing web based tools to detect sea levels. In 2017, The country decided to invest in meteorology network to improve weather forecasting. By investing in this Technology, The Bahamas was provided with both hardware and weather radars. This will assist in preparing for any future disaster and to prevent any damages.

Bahama's goals implementing new technology
  • Positive impact on disaster management
  • Tracking Sea Levels
